[TITLE] LEAF: Carbon Footprint Estimation for Cloud Deployments Before You Deploy
[TAG] Research
[CONTENT] Cloud infrastructure is a growing contributor to global carbon emissions, yet most sustainability tools only kick in after a system is already running. LEAF is a lightweight framework developed with Lloyds Offshore Global Services that flips this around, estimating carbon footprint from Terraform configurations before anything is deployed.

The framework uses a three-layer approach: it parses Infrastructure-as-Code definitions, simulates expected workload behavior, and pulls in region-specific carbon intensity data to produce energy and emission estimates without needing access to application source code. Evaluated across three real-world projects, LEAF's simulations land within 15.7% of post-deployment measurements under realistic workloads, giving architects and developers a practical tool for making sustainability-informed decisions early in the provisioning process.
